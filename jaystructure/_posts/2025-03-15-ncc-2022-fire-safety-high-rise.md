The 2022 edition of the National Construction Code (NCC) brought some of the most significant updates to fire safety provisions in over a decade. For anyone working on high-rise Class 2 buildings — apartments, specifically — understanding these changes isn't optional.

## What Changed in NCC 2022

The 2022 update consolidated and restructured Volume One significantly. For fire safety in high-rise residential, the key changes fell across three areas:

### 1. Sprinkler System Requirements

NCC 2022 extended mandatory sprinkler requirements. For Class 2 buildings over a certain effective height, full sprinkler coverage is now required across more building elements, including some areas previously exempt under older editions.

The practical implication: coordinate with your hydraulic engineer earlier in the design phase. Sprinkler head placement affects ceiling void depths, which affects your structural slab-to-slab heights, which affects everything downstream.

### 2. Smoke Hazard Management

The smoke hazard management provisions were rewritten for clarity. Section C (formerly Section E) now specifies performance requirements more explicitly, which gives designers more flexibility but also more obligation to demonstrate compliance.

On complex projects, this often means a smoke modelling report from a fire engineer — factor that into your programme.

### 3. Exit Travel Distances

Exit travel distance requirements were tightened for some building configurations. If you're working from a design that was approved under NCC 2019, don't assume the travel distances still comply. Run the numbers again under the current edition.

## What This Means on Site

Understanding the code is one thing. Applying it during construction is another.

The most common site issues I see on high-rise residential:

- **Penetration sealing** — Every service penetration through a fire-rated wall or floor must be sealed to the same fire-resistance level. This is often missed on fast-moving projects.
- **Doorset compliance** — Fire doors in egress paths must be self-closing, not held open. Trades prop them constantly. Document it and close them out.
- **Compartmentation integrity** — If structural works create any new opening in a fire-rated element, it must be reinstated before the area is handed over.

## Key References

- NCC 2022 Volume One, Section C — Fire Resistance
- AS 1428 — Sprinkler systems
- AS/NZS 2293 — Emergency escape lighting

If you're preparing a project-specific fire safety strategy, always engage a fire engineer early. The code is a minimum — good design goes further.
